About Me

Background

I’m Emily Onofrio, a third-year Software Engineering student at UC Irvine, with an expected graduation date of March 2028. I transferred to UCI after earning associate degrees in Mathematics and Computer Science, which gave me a strong analytical foundation and a deep appreciation for problem-solving.

Technical Interests

I’m particularly interested in backend development for healthcare-focused applications. I’m drawn to work that happens behind the scenes—building reliable systems, handling data responsibly, and supporting tools that make a real-world impact without necessarily being visible to end users.

Alongside backend development, I enjoy web design and exploring applied AI. I like thinking about how systems are experienced from both the technical and user-facing sides, and how intelligent tools can be integrated thoughtfully into software rather than used for novelty.

Outside of Class

In my free time, I enjoy working on personal coding projects where I can experiment, learn new tools, and refine ideas iteratively. I value writing clean, understandable code and enjoy the process of debugging, refactoring, and improving systems over time.

Outside of coursework, I’m interested in the technical side of photography, particularly understanding how tools and settings affect outcomes. I enjoy hobbies that combine creativity with technical depth, which mirrors how I approach software engineering.
